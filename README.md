# GSIS and SSS Compensation Analysis

### Business Task
Analyze trends in Employees’ Compensation (EC) claims, benefits paid, and contributions collected by Government Service Insurance System (GSIS) and Social Security System (SSS) from 1975 to 2023 to assess the financial sustainability of the EC program.

### Data Set
- This dataset provides a historical overview of Employees’ Compensation (EC) program performance of 1975 to 2023 from [Open Data Philippines](https://data.gov.ph/index/public/resource/compendium-of-employees'-compensation-%28ec%29-statistics/number-of-ec-claims,-amount-paid-and-contribution-collected-from-gsis-and-sss-%281975-june-2023%29/zl21lq6x-0aq8-njpa-btd5-f5cuvab7wpfq)

  - Slicers

### Key Insights

**Average Payout per Claim**

<img width="603" height="497" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/bc5fb24c-9661-4218-9690-5b964fa04060" />

- The analysis shows a significant spike in 2003, where the average payout per claim reached approximately PHP 206,375.6, which is notably higher than surrounding years and deviates from the overall trend.

- This anomaly likely indicates a data quality issue, potentially due to underreported or missing values in the number of claims, which inflates the computed average.

- Excluding this outlier, the average payout per claim remains relatively stable over time, suggesting consistent benefit levels and controlled payout structures.

**Payout-to-Contribution Ratio**

<img width="565" height="292" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/29da575a-56cf-438a-8a5b-0b211bff24e5" />

- The data indicates that the SSS payout-to-contribution ratio was elevated between 1994 and 2007, suggesting that payouts consumed a larger proportion of collected contributions during this period.

- This trend may reflect reduced financial sustainability, as higher ratios imply tighter margins between inflows and outflows.

- A similar pattern is observed for GSIS, where the payout-to-contribution ratio remains relatively high from 1984 to 2002, indicating a prolonged period of lower financial efficiency.

- These periods highlight potential structural or economic factors that placed pressure on both systems’ financial balance.

**Contributions Over Time**

<img width="563" height="347" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/e82f314f-7aae-4fde-b49e-af92bde9855c" />

- This sustained increase suggests an expanding contribution pool, which is critical in supporting long-term system sustainability.
